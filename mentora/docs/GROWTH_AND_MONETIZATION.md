| **T2 — Semi-urban aspirational** | Tier-2 cities (Jaipur, Lucknow, Indore, Patna, Surat, Coimbatore) | ₹40K–₹1L | ₹1,000–₹2,500/mo; price-sensitive but education-priority | Local tuition quality gap; regional language AI tutor; affordable Scholar plan |
| **T3 — Small town / rural** | Tier-3+ towns, rural | ₹15K–₹40K | ₹300–₹800/mo; very price-sensitive | Explorer (free) as entry; WhatsApp-native teacher discovery; Hindi/vernacular AI tutor |
| **Diaspora — Canada/US/UK/AU** | NRI/PIO communities | CAD/USD/GBP equivalent ≥ USD 80K/yr | USD $19–$39/mo (US base rate); pays full price; high trust in Indian-origin teachers | Want Indian curriculum connection; cultural continuity; retired Indian professors as tutors |
| **Global South expansion** | UAE, SG, PH, NG, KE, ZA, EG | Varies | See PPP_PRICING.md | Large Indian diaspora (UAE, SG) + local demand for affordable English-medium tutoring |

**Why now (demand triggers, 2025–2026):**
1. India's private tutoring market ~USD 4.1B (IMARC) growing at ~7.5% CAGR; online tutoring segment forecast USD 27B by 2029 at ~26% CAGR (Technavio).
2. BYJU'S insolvency created a ~20M-student trust vacuum. Parents are actively looking for a transparent, cancel-anytime alternative. ([Inc42, 2024](https://inc42.com/features/byjus-2024-review-collapse-edtech-giant-byju-raveendran/))
3. Board exam pressure is structural and permanent. ~92% of secondary students sit state boards; supplemental tutoring demand spikes Oct–Feb annually.
4. India's edtech market valued at ~USD 3.63B in 2025, projected to reach ~USD 33B by 2034 at ~28% CAGR (IMARC). K-12 holds ~43% share in 2025. ([IMARC EdTech India](https://www.imarcgroup.com/india-edtech-market))
5. Asia Pacific leads global online tutoring with ~35.5% revenue share; 55%+ of Indian students engage with private tutoring. ([Grand View Research](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/online-tutoring-services-market))
6. India's silver economy (60+ population ~104M, growing) means an increasing pool of retired professionals seeking meaningful, income-generating activity — the supply side is structurally ready.

## 3. Retention & Outcomes

A marketplace lives or dies by repeat purchase. Mentora's retention levers map directly to in-app features (built or near-term).

| Retention Lever | In-App Feature | Why It Works | Metric Targeted |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Learning streaks + XP** | `streak_days`, `xp_points`, `badges` fields on UserProfile (built) | Gamification drives daily habit; streaks create loss-aversion; kids do not want to break a 14-day streak | DAU/WAU ratio; session frequency |
| **Progress dashboard for parents** | Guardian dashboard with session count, AI tutor usage, quiz scores, board exam countdown | Post-BYJU'S parents need to see value tangibly; weekly email/WhatsApp digest builds trust | Guardian login rate; churn reduction |
| **Outcome tracking — board exam improvement** | Post-exam score entry by student; teacher tags "exam prep" sessions; end-of-term report | "Mentora student improved from 67% to 84% in Class 10 Maths" is the most powerful testimonial/case study possible | NPS; testimonial collection rate; renewal rate |
| **Teacher ratings + reviews** | 5-star rating + written review post-session (built: `reviews` in schema) | Quality signal for next parent; social proof flywheel; also motivates teachers to deliver | Review rate; average rating; booking conversion |
| **AI tutor homework help** | `tutor_chat` AI task (built); multilingual (Hindi/Bengali/Punjabi, near-term) | Student who gets unstuck at 10pm via AI tutor has reason to return tomorrow; AI usage is a strong early retention signal | AI sessions/week; correlation with 30-day retention |
| **Cohort group classes** | Group `ClassSession` with up to N students (built schema); teacher creates a "Class 10 Maths cohort" | Cohort = social commitment; students do not want to let the group down; also improves teacher economics (1 slot → multiple revenues) | Group booking rate; cohort completion rate |
| **Seasonal exam-prep sprints** | Oct–Feb: "Board Exam Bootcamp" bundle (10 sessions + AI mock tests + teacher feedback); marketed as a time-limited cohort | Urgency + outcome focus; drives lapsed-user reactivation; annual revenue spike | Bootcamp conversion; reactivation rate |
| **Parent digest (weekly WhatsApp/email)** | Auto-generated weekly summary: "Priya attended 3 sessions this week. Quiz score improved by 12%. Next session: Mon 6pm with Ramesh Sir." | Keeps parents engaged between sessions; reinforces perceived value; prompts renewal | Open rate; churn delta for digest recipients |
| **Referral loop** | In-app referral: "Your friend gets 1 month free, you get 1 month free" (needs build) | Happy parent is a free sales rep; referrals arrive pre-sold on trust; referred users churn less | Referral rate; referred-user LTV vs. organic |
| **Teacher loyalty + earnings transparency** | Teacher earnings dashboard: total earned, upcoming payouts, top-performing sessions (needs build) | Retained teachers = retained students; teacher who earns ₹15,000/mo is not going to leave; supply retention is demand retention | Teacher monthly earnings; teacher 90-day churn |

## 4. Monetization — Revenue Stacks

### 4.1 Subscription + Marketplace Hybrid

Mentora earns from two overlapping streams that compound:

| Stream | Mechanism | Mentora Take | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Subscription (student/family)** | Monthly/annual fee for platform access, AI tutor, AI homework help, group class discovery | 100% of subscription fee | Recurring, predictable; decoupled from session volume |
| **Marketplace commission** | Teacher sets a session rate; student books and pays; Mentora takes a % | 15–20% of session GMV (see take-rate strategy below) | Scales with session volume; incentivises supply quality |
| **B2B school plan** | School or coaching centre pays annual licence for N student seats + admin dashboard + bulk AI tools | 100% of licence; no commission on sessions within the plan | Predictable ARR; high LTV; lower CAC via school-as-channel |
| **Mentor Pro (teacher subscription)** | Retired teacher pays ₹999/mo to access full AI tooling: lesson plan generator, quiz generator, research briefing, OCR, analytics | 100% of teacher subscription | Becomes negative-marginal-cost once teacher earns >₹5,000/mo on platform; very high net revenue margin |

### 4.2 Plan Pricing (India + Global Base)

| Plan | India INR/mo | India INR/yr | US USD/mo | Target Persona |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Explorer** | Free | Free | Free | Try-before-buy; student discovery |
| **Scholar** (1 child, full AI + live sessions) | ₹1,499 | ₹14,990 (~₹1,249/mo) | $19 | Urban/semi-urban single-child family; competitive with 1 subject local tuition |
| **Family** (up to 4 children + Guardian dashboard) | ₹2,999 | ₹27,999 (~₹2,333/mo) | $39 | Families with multiple school-age children; strong value vs. paying per subject |
| **Mentor Pro** (teacher subscription) | ₹999 | ₹8,990 (~₹749/mo) | $12 | Retired teacher; pays for AI tooling, course builder, analytics |

**Annual discount logic:** 17% off (Scholar), 19% off (Family) on annual plans — strong enough to shift purchase intent, not so large it destroys monthly ARPU. Annual buyers have 2–3× lower churn.

**PPP/regional pricing:** See PPP_PRICING.md for the full implementation-ready table covering ~21 countries. PPP pricing lifts conversion in India/South Asia/Africa/SEA by pricing to local purchasing power without reducing ARPU for high-income markets (US/CA/GB/AU/UAE/SG pay full base rate).

### 4.3 Teacher Take-Rate Strategy

**Target:** 80% teacher payout / 20% Mentora commission at launch; move to 82/18 at 1,000+ active teachers (volume signal to attract supply).

**Rationale:**
- Outschool (closest structural analog) charges teachers 40% for organic discovery, 30% for self-promoted. At 80/20, Mentora is significantly more teacher-friendly — a supply-side moat.
- Retired teachers who earn ₹10,000–₹25,000/month on Mentora will actively refer colleagues and defend the platform. High take-rates (as Unacademy uses) create resentful supply.
- At scale (GMV ₹50Cr/yr), 20% take = ₹10Cr commission revenue — meaningful without being exploitative.

**Rate card at launch:**
- Sessions booked via organic search/discovery within Mentora: 20% commission.
- Sessions booked via teacher's own shared link (teacher-led acquisition): 15% commission.
- Group classes (teacher has >3 students/session): 18% commission (slightly reduced to incentivise groups which improve teacher economics).

### 4.4 PPP / Region-Based Pricing — Why It Lifts Revenue Without Cannibalisation

The core insight ([Dodo Payments](https://dodopayments.com/blogs/purchasing-power-parity-pricing-saas), [Mirava](https://www.mirava.io/blog/how-to-set-up-purchasing-power-parity-pricing)): charging $19/mo to an Indian family earning ₹40,000/mo is like charging a US family $250/mo. The result is no conversion at all, which means $0 revenue — not $19.

**Anti-cannibalisation mechanics:**
- Currency is detected by payment-method country at checkout (card issuing bank country), not by VPN or IP.
- Manual override allowed (expat in India who wants to pay USD is permitted; they just pay USD).
- Pricing is not publicly crosslinked between regions (India landing page shows ₹ only; US shows USD only).
- Annual plans in India/South Asia are still far cheaper than a single session with a local tutor → no Indian user would pay USD pricing.

Companies implementing localized pricing see approximately 30% higher growth rates ([Mirava](https://www.mirava.io/blog/regional-pricing-subscriptions-best-practices)). Spotify India premium at ~$3.50/mo (vs $9.99 US) drove massive subscriber growth without undermining US pricing. Netflix India mobile-only plan at ~$2.70/mo accounted for ~50% of new subscriber additions after launch.

| Tier | Description | Pricing Model | Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| **School Lite** | Up to 100 student seats; teacher profiles browsable by school counsellor; no branded LMS | ₹2,00,000/yr (~₹2,000/student seat) | Tier-2/3 private schools; coaching centres |
| **School Standard** | 101–500 students; admin dashboard; bulk AI quiz/lesson plan; integration with school notice board | ₹6,00,000/yr | Mid-size private schools; franchise coaching chains |

## 6. KPIs & Risks

### 6.1 North Star & Operating KPIs

| KPI | Definition | Target (Year 1 end) | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Activation rate** | Free → paid conversion within 14 days | 8–12% | Measures product-market fit signal |
| **WAU/MAU ratio** | Weekly active users / monthly active users | ≥ 0.45 | Stickiness; below 0.3 = engagement problem |
| **Paid conversion** | Explorer → any paid plan | 5–8% of registered users | Revenue efficiency |
| **D30 retention** | % of new paying subscribers still active on Day 30 | ≥ 65% | Early churn predictor |
| **M6 retention** | % of cohort still paying after 6 months | ≥ 45% | LTV predictor; benchmark for K-12 EdTech |
| **NPS (parents)** | Net Promoter Score from post-session survey | ≥ 55 | Referral engine health; trust signal |
| **Teacher utilisation** | Avg booked hours/teacher/week | ≥ 5 hrs by Month 6 | Supply-side health; below 3 = demand problem or mismatch |
| **GMV** | Gross session transaction value (INR) | ₹50L in Month 12 | Marketplace traction |
| **Take-rate realized** | Actual commission / GMV | 18–20% | Revenue quality |
| **CAC payback** | Months to recover CAC from gross margin | ≤ 3 months (India) | Capital efficiency |
| **Blended LTV:CAC** | Gross margin × lifetime / acquisition cost | ≥ 5:1 | Investment case signal |

### 6.2 Key Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Supply-side cold start** — not enough quality retired teachers at launch to satisfy demand | High | High | Pre-recruit 50 teachers before launch via retired teacher associations, WhatsApp outreach, and pension board networks; offer guaranteed ₹500 "onboarding bonus" for first completed session profile |
| **Teacher tech-literacy barrier** — retired teachers cannot navigate the platform | Medium | High | AI-assisted onboarding wizard; "If you can use WhatsApp, you can teach on Mentora" messaging; WhatsApp helpline staffed by Mentora support during first 90 days |
| **Low paid conversion from free** — Explorer tier is too generous | Medium | Medium | Time-limit AI tutor messages on free tier (e.g., 5/day); show upgrade prompt at natural moments (student asks question #6, parent views second teacher profile) |
| **Post-BYJU'S trust overhang** — parents refuse any EdTech subscription | Medium | High | Strict cancel-anytime messaging on all pricing pages; no annual lock-in without explicit consent; money-back guarantee for first 30 days |
| **Marketplace leakage** — teachers and students transact off-platform after first session | Medium | High | Build real value in platform (session recordings, progress dashboards, AI tools, payment protection); make off-platform transaction riskier (no Mentora guarantee if session goes off-platform) |
| **Vernacular AI quality** — Hindi/Bengali AI tutor gives poor answers | Medium | High | Run internal QA red-team of 10 native speakers per language before launch; build teacher escalation path if AI gives wrong answer |
| **B2B sales cycle too long** — schools take 6–12 months to decide | Medium | Medium | Lead with a free "school partnership" that costs schools nothing (revenue share model instead of licence); prove value on 20 pilot students before asking for contract |
| **Regulatory: DPDP Act** — children's data fines | Low | Very High | Build DPDP compliance before launch (parental consent gate, DPO appointment, data minimisation); see DIFFERENTIATION.md §5 |
| **Take-rate compression** — teachers demand lower commission as platform grows | Low | Medium | Lock in 80/20 split in ToS; position as the most teacher-friendly platform (vs Outschool's 40% take); teacher community forum to surface concerns before they become churn |
| **Global expansion distraction** — Canada/US pulls focus from India core | Low | Medium | Global is a natural harvest of diaspora demand with zero product changes needed (English UI, USD Stripe already exists); assign one growth person to diaspora channels; no new product work required until India GMV > ₹5Cr/mo |
